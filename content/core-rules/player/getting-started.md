Heroic Tales is designed for a group of one to six players plus one GM. More than six players is a big group and, especially with the collaborative nature of storytelling in this system, can result in people feeling left out or unheard.

One person in the group needs to assume the role of Game Master (GM). You, lucky person, will provide a loose plot (since no plot survives contact with the players unscathed), set the scene, and narrate the outcomes of dice rolls for the rest of the people in your group. You are responsible for playing all of the characters, creatures, and intelligent super-storms that the player characters will encounter on their adventures.

Everyone else assumes the role of a player and will take on the persona of a character for the duration of the game. As a group of players, the first step is to collaboratively create your team concept based upon the shared premise for your campaign. You might be playing a class of student superheroes in Hero Town; a band of zombie-plague survivors barely hanging onto sanity in Run for Your Life; a team of sneaky ninja in Super Secret Ninja Team, Go!; or the crew of a giant, bronze, animated statue in Colossus vs. Kaiju.

Based on the team concept, each player will create the details of their team member. You’ll need to think about your character’s appearance, personality, and skills, powers, and interests.

## Team
One player should record the following information about the team:

* **Name** — maybe you’re a group of adventurers known as the Perilous Peregrine Questing Company or a guild of ninja known as the Azure Hand. Perhaps you’re a team of superheroes known as the Wildcats or part of a group of villains that the media has dubbed the Terrible Ten. Even if you don’t use it in character, give yourselves a team name. It’s more fun that way.
* **Origin story** — why is your team a team? How did you all come to meet and how did you figure out how to effectively work together?
* **Power level** — every team starts at a *power level* of 1. This will increase over time as your team becomes more effective at working together.
* **Momentum** — this numeric value represents your team’s ability to overcome adversity. You will begin each *challenge* with an amount of *momentum* based on your team size. Depending on your dice rolls, your momentum may decrease during play, but it will never increase above this value.

## Character
Each player should then record the following about their character:

* **Name** — your name should be appropriate to the setting. If you’re struggling for ideas, try a random name generator or ask your group for help.
* **Appearance** — what does your character look like? How do they dress? What equipment are they carrying? Do they have any specific physical mannerisms that others would notice?
* **Personality** — what motivates you? What makes you sad? What are your hopes and fears? How do you interact with other people? With animals? With intelligent cyclones?

### Traits
The following *traits* will factor into play when your team is tackling a challenge, the core mechanic of Heroic Tales. By weaving details of your character’s *skills*, *powers*, and *interests* into the description of your actions, you can influence the size of your dice pool.

**Skills** — list three talents that you have spent time practicing, but which don’t generally factor into combat. For example: calligraphy, rock climbing, alchemy, ballroom dancing, or calculus.

**Powers** — list three abilities that you possess innately or which you have acquired through other means, such as magic rings, radioactive spiders, or gamma-ray bursts. These are often abilities that can be used in combat. For example: super-strength, archery, jujutsu, necromancy, fencing, or invisibility.

**Interests** — list three subjects that you know more about than most other people. For example: particle physics, occult languages, the economy of dwarven nations, or xenobiology.
