Here are a few things that your character, as a native to the fantastic world of Gilea, would know.

## A Brief History of Time
Before the beginning of time, before the creation of sunlit plains and shadowed valleys, before the birth of spirits and the worlds they inhabit, was the *Dao* — silent, eternal, and unchanging.

Out of *wuji*, the formless void, emerged *taiji*, the potential for everything. Taiji split into *yin* and *yang*, the two fundamental forces. 

Yin and yang are always transforming and their constant movement molded *qi*, the vital energy that suffuses all matter, into the myriad aspects of the multiverse.

Where qi was light and insubstantial, the Aether formed. Where qi was heavy and solid, the many worlds formed. Where qi accumulated, there was life. Where it dissipated, there was death. The forces of yin and yang, constantly in motion, created the myriad spirits and creatures that inhabit each of the worlds.

## A Post-War World
Before the War of Ages, the mighty empires maintained peace through diplomacy, trade, and a strong system of international cooperation. However, when the world plunged into chaos after the opening of the Demon Gate, the international order quickly collapsed.

Cities crumbled, farmlands fell barren, and pestilence spread through the mountains, forests, and coastal plains. The ascended species broke into a multitude of factions and fought over what fertile and defensible land was left.

Over the course of 300 years, ninety percent of the ascended species perished. During this period of tumult, nations rose and fell, civilizations disappeared, and much of the land was reclaimed by the natural world.

Eventually, human, elven, and dwarven leaders forged an alliance which ended the conflict and closed the Demon Gate, returning a semblance of peace to the world. Now, adventuring companies sponsor excursions into the wilds in order to discover the ruins of the past, recover lost artifacts and knowledge, and spread civilization throughout Gilea.

## An Animistic World
Gilea is a world filled with spirits. They inhabit and animate mountains, lakes, rivers, trees, clouds, fields, and waterfalls. They cause the rain to fall, the rivers to flow, the wind to gust, and the crops to grow. They are the forces of nature, they are everywhere, and keeping them happy is the key to ensuring good fortune.

The key difference from other settings is that there are no creator deities. The spirits of the sunlit and shadow worlds may be very powerful, but they don’t serve the same role that gods do in other settings.

## A Magical World
Gilea is filled with magical spells, powers, items, and effects. In this world, magic is the result of manipulating qi, the vital energy that permeates all things. Some creatures entreat spirits to manipulate qi on their behalf while others are able to manipulate it directly. Some creatures produce magical effects by learning to control the qi within themselves and others produce them by using magical weapons, armor, and other wondrous items.

Everyone on Gilea expects to see and use magic in their everyday lives. Most peasants have one or two common magical items in their possession, shamans offer basic medical care, and hedge mages use low-level magic to provide useful services.

## Portals & Pocket Dimensions
The multiverse that contains Gilea has a fractal structure. Each material world has a multitude of shadow and sunlit worlds that surround it. As the number of spirits continues to increase, the number of outer worlds grows as well.

In addition, Gilea is filled with pocket dimensions — worlds within worlds. The space inside an old town well may be ten times the size it should be and contain an ancient slumbering horror. Alternately, a shrine to a spirit may contain an entire spirit world inside it.

Gilea has a multitude of gates and portals that connect it to the other worlds. Some of these portals are easily discernible, while others blend into the landscape. Some take no effort to navigate, while others require considerable effort.
