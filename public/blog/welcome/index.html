Welcome

November 03, 2020

Welcome to Heroic Tales!

I didn’t set out to write a new system. I have three kids under five, and I wanted to spend some quality time with them playing table-top RPGs. I tried D&D 5E, which I play regularly with my friends, but even a streamlined version of that system was a bit too weighty for my needs.

As I was looking around for other systems to try out with the kids, though, I became inspired. I’m a professional tech person and a forever DM, so designing a custom rule set felt pretty natural. I’m no stranger to number crunching to work out balance issues, and I knew the flavor and feel of what would work well both for my kids and for some lightweight side-quest sessions with my regular gaming group.

Once I started, it unlocked a tsunami of creative ideas that had been bottled up in favor of the crunchy, tactical, mechanics heavy systems I’d been playing. The system has gone through a lot of balancing, play testing, and feedback, and I’m using it successfully to game with my kids and to supplement my regular gaming group’s D&D sessions.

I’ve intentionally designed Heroic Tales to be modular and extensible. The core mechanics are pretty solid, but it exists to be hacked, tweaked, flavored, and reimplemented in a variety of ways. To encourage reuse, I’m releasing it under a Creative Commons license, which means that both GMs and designers can take as little or as much as they want from the Core Rules (and any supplements I create).

Hopefully, this genre-neutral, rules light, narrative system of heroic problem solving will give you as much joy to play as it gave me to design.
